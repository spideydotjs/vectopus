# Planned Proposed Production Lines (2017 Strategic Planning)

This document provides a detailed technical overview, process description, core machinery list, and estimated cost analysis for the five proposed production lines planned in 2017 for polyurethane (PU) and polyisocyanurate (PIR) foam insulation products.

---

## 1. Continuous Double Belt Production Lines for PIR/PUR Solid Insulation Panels

This line is designed for the high-volume, continuous production of rigid insulation panels featuring a PIR/PUR core with flexible or rigid claddings (e.g., aluminum foil, kraft paper, fiberglass, or prepainted steel sheets).

### A. Core Machinery & Components
* **Cladding Unwinding & Feeding System:**
  * Dual-cantilever decoilers with automatic splicing units for continuous feeding.
  * Tension control systems and alignment guides.
* **Pre-heating & Conditioning Oven:**
  * Multi-zone IR/convective air preheating tunnels to bring cladding to 45°C - 60°C to optimize foam adhesion.
* **High-Pressure PU/PIR Foaming System:**
  * High-pressure metering pumps (Rexroth or equivalent) with closed-loop flow control.
  * Multi-component mixing head (handling Polyol, Isocyanate, Catalyst, Flame Retardants, and Pentane blowing agent).
  * Oscillating pour-stringer/dispenser (gantry-mounted) for even liquid distribution.
* **Double Belt Conveyor (Slat Conveyor):**
  * Length: 24 to 36 meters (critical for curing times at high speeds).
  * High-precision heated steel slats with digital temperature control (up to 70°C).
  * Side containment belts (side blocks) with automatic width/thickness adjustment.
* **Cutting & Profiling Unit:**
  * Flying band saw or circular saw cutter synced with conveyor line speed.
  * Cross-cut and edge-trimming systems.
* **Handling & Packaging Line:**
  * Cooling wheel (for outgassing and cooling).
  * Stacker (vacuum lift or fork type).
  * Stretch-wrapping and strapping machines.

### B. Technical Specifications
* **Production Speed:** 5 to 15 m/min (extendable up to 30 m/min with longer conveyor).
* **Panel Thickness Range:** 20 mm to 200 mm.
* **Panel Width:** 1000 mm to 1200 mm.
* **Core Foam Density:** 35 to 45 kg/m³.
* **Blowing Agent:** Cyclopentane (requires integrated gas safety/monitoring sensors).

### C. Process Flow
1. Cladding is decoiled, aligned, and pre-heated.
2. Liquid PU/PIR chemicals are mixed under high pressure (150–200 bar) and distributed onto the bottom cladding.
3. The foaming mixture enters the heated double belt conveyor, where it expands, filling the cavity between top and bottom cladding, and cures under pressure.
4. The continuous cured panel is cut into desired lengths by the flying saw.
5. Finished panels are cooled, stacked, wrapped, and palletized.

### D. Estimated Budget & CAPEX (2017)
* **Equipment Cost:** €1,800,000 – €3,500,000 (depending on conveyor length and level of automation).
* **Safety & Pentane Penthouse System:** €150,000 – €250,000.

## 2. Structural Insulated Panels (SIPs) Manufacturing Lines

This line is configured for the discontinuous production of structural panels used in modular building, consisting of an Oriented Strand Board (OSB) skin on both sides and an insulating core (EPS, XPS, or PU).

### A. Core Machinery & Components
* **OSB Board Preparation & Feeding Station:**
  * Automated vacuum loading arms for OSB sheet handling.
  * Feed roller conveyors with centering guides.
* **Adhesive Application System (Glue Spreader):**
  * Multi-nozzle gantry applicator for one-part polyurethane adhesive.
  * Water mist misting station (necessary to activate PU moisture-cure glues).
* **Core Placement Table:**
  * Manual or automated station for aligning pre-cut EPS/XPS boards onto the glued OSB.
* **Lamination Press:**
  * Multi-opening hydraulic press or heavy-duty vacuum press.
  * Platen dimensions: up to 3.0 m × 6.0 m.
  * Pressing force: 5 to 10 metric tons/m².
* **CNC Machining Center:**
  * Portal-style 3-axis CNC router for cutting windows, doors, and routering connecting splines (SIP joints).

### B. Technical Specifications
* **Panel Dimensions:** Width up to 1250 mm; Length up to 6000 mm.
* **Core Thickness Range:** 50 mm to 300 mm.
* **Cycle Time:** 5 to 10 minutes per press load (depending on adhesive curing characteristics).
* **Daily Capacity:** 80 to 120 panels per shift.

### C. Process Flow
1. Bottom OSB sheet is fed onto the conveyor and coated with PU adhesive.
2. The insulation core (EPS/XPS) is placed on the adhesive layer.
3. Top OSB sheet is coated with adhesive and laid over the core.
4. The assembled sandwich is moved into the hydraulic press, where pressure is applied until the glue cures.
5. The panel is discharged, transferred to the CNC router for edge profiling/detail cuts, and stacked.

### D. Estimated Budget & CAPEX (2017)
* **Equipment Cost:** €220,000 – €550,000 (higher range includes automated portal CNC routers).

## 3. PU/PIR Spray Foam Equipment (Field Application)

Portable, high-pressure proportioning systems designed for on-site application of polyurethane foam (insulating walls, roofs, tanks, and cold storage facilities).

### A. Core Machinery & Components
* **High-Pressure Proportioner (Reactor):**
  * Hydraulic or electric motor-driven dual piston pumps.
  * Primary heaters (up to 10–12 kW per side) with precise digital temperature controls.
* **Heated Hose Assembly:**
  * Length: 15 to 90 meters.
  * Integrated copper heating elements and thermocouple sensors for temperature maintenance.
* **Spray Gun:**
  * Chamber-direct impingement mixing guns (e.g., Graco Fusion AP or ClearShot).
  * Easy-clean air-purge or mechanical-purge designs.
* **Material Transfer Pumps:**
  * 2:1 ratio pneumatic drum pumps for feeding raw materials from 55-gallon drums.

### B. Technical Specifications
* **Max Output:** 9 to 18 kg/min (20 to 40 lbs/min).
* **Max Working Pressure:** 140 to 240 bar (2000 to 3500 psi).
* **Max Material Temperature:** 88°C (190°F).
* **Power Requirements:** 3-phase 380V or single-phase 220V generators.

### C. Process Flow
1. Drum pumps transfer Polyol and Isocyanate from raw drums to the proportioner.
2. The proportioner heats the two components individually to lower viscosity.
3. The materials are pressurized and sent through heated hoses to prevent temperature drop.
4. The two components mix inside the spray gun chamber at the point of exit and are sprayed onto the substrate, where they react and expand instantly.

### D. Estimated Budget & CAPEX (2017)
* **Equipment Cost:** €8,500 – €25,000 per complete mobile setup.

## 4. PU/PIR Pipe Insulation (Shell/Section) Production Lines

A specialized molding line for manufacturing cylindrical pipe insulation sections used in district heating, petrochemical pipelines, and HVAC systems.

### A. Core Machinery & Components
* **Pipe Shell Mold Carousels/Tables:**
  * Multi-cavity aluminum or steel molds with core mandrels representing standard pipe diameters.
  * Clamping locks (manual or pneumatic/hydraulic).
* **Low/High-Pressure PU Dispenser:**
  * Output range: 30 to 100 kg/min.
  * Mixing head mounted on a movable arm/gantry.
* **Mold Temperature Control Units:**
  * Water circulation heaters to maintain molds at 45°C - 50°C.
* **Post-processing Saws:**
  * Longitudinal and circular cutting saws for splitting shells into semi-cylinders (half-shells) and trimming ends.

### B. Technical Specifications
* **Pipe Diameter Capacity:** DN 15 mm to DN 500 mm.
* **Shell Length:** Standard 1.0 m or 1.2 m.
* **Density:** 40 to 80 kg/m³.
* **Curing Cycle Time:** 3 to 6 minutes.

### C. Process Flow
1. Molds are sprayed with release agent and closed around the inner core mandrel.
2. Liquid polyurethane is injected through the gate of the mold.
3. Foam expands symmetrically around the mandrel and cures.
4. Molds are opened, pipe shells are extracted, and sent to the saw line for splitting and trimming.

### D. Estimated Budget & CAPEX (2017)
* **Equipment Cost:** €90,000 – €280,000 (depending on the number of mold stations and automation level of the carousel).

## 5. Rigid PU/PIR Block Production Lines

This system is configured for producing large blocks of rigid polyurethane or polyisocyanurate foam, which are later sliced into sheets, panels, or custom CNC profiles.

### A. Core Machinery & Components
* **Molding Box (Discontinuous Block Mold):**
  * Heavy-duty steel structural box with side doors that open pneumatically.
  * Dimensions: e.g., 1.25 m × 1.25 m × 4.0 m or 1.0 m × 1.2 m × 2.4 m.
* **High-Output Chemical Dispenser:**
  * Output capacity: 150 to 300 kg/min.
  * Overhead crane/manipulator to swing the mix head evenly across the mold floor.
* **Slicing & Cutting Line:**
  * Horizontal band saws for slicing blocks into sheets.
  * Vertical band saws for edge squaring and sizing.
  * Profile CNC wire/oscillating band saws for custom 3D shapes.

### B. Technical Specifications
* **Block Dimensions:** Up to 1.25 m height × 1.25 m width × 4.0 m length.
* **Foam Density Range:** 30 to 150 kg/m³ (high density blocks used for tooling or structural supports).
* **Production Rate:** 4 to 8 blocks per hour.

### C. Process Flow
1. The inner mold surfaces are lined with kraft paper or coated with release agent.
2. The dispenser pours a calculated shot of mixed PU/PIR liquid into the bottom of the mold.
3. The mold is locked, and the foam rises vertically under controlled venting.
4. After curing (approx. 15–20 mins), the mold doors open, and the block is extracted.
5. The block is stored for 24 hours to cure fully and dissipate heat, then sent to the slicing line.

### D. Estimated Budget & CAPEX (2017)
* **Equipment Cost:** €150,000 – €480,000 (including advanced horizontal/vertical band saws).
